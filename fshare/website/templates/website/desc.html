It's a minimalist and *partially secured* file sharing website. Just drop your file and send a link. No registration needed.

It's a one-shot upload, that means that a file can only be downloaded once. Then, it is removed from our disks (yes, completely). Files are only persistent for 7 days, after what they are deleted from our disks (yes, completely).

Download links are made of 12 random characters in a charset of 62 items, that is about 70 bits of entropy. This makes the guess of download links very unlinkely. In addition, you can protect your file by a key, which is another protection against other users. So yes, your files are safe relatively to other users.

Not entirely. Your files are stored encrypted (using the key you provided at upload) on the server, and the server does not store the key. But, due to performance caring and technical specifitities, the encryption is handled by the server. So, in an obvious way, it knows at some point both the key and the clear file. We are working on client-side encryption and decryption to improve this point.

Clone FShare and install it on your own server. You can even ask for a sub-domain of fshare.io.

Registration is a mechanism to gain more freedom on your use of FShare. For instance, you can set the number of downloads before deleting the file (up to a bound), or the expiration date of your file (also up to a bound). You also have a higher limit for each file.

Nope. To be a registered user, you need a key provided by the owner of the website (in this case, us). This key corresponds to a certain class of restrictions that sets the bounds for your account. To sum up, bounds depend on the registration key you have.
